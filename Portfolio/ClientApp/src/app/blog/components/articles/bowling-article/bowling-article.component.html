Tracking Bowling Scores and Visualizing Interesting Statistics October 2, 2019 A page on my website for my friends and I to enter our bowling scores and calculate relevant statistics. The Idea In April 2019, a few friends and I decided to go bowling for fun. It was a pretty common thing for us to do, maybe once or twice a year. This time, though, we decided we’d get ever-so-slightly more serious about it and start bowling regularly. Naturally, I started taking pictures of every game we bowled so I could see how we all improved as we started getting better. It wasn’t long before I realized this was a perfect opportunity to build something that would actually be useful - and not only to me, but to all of my friends who bowled as well.
Getting to Work Once I got identity and authentication set up for my speedrunning project I couldn’t wait to start working on the bowling app. By this point, I had racked up nearly three months worth of bowling scores (the more dedicated of us were bowling 3-4 times a week, so it was adding up) and I wanted the ability to at least start adding the scores to a database. I looked to online bowling score calculators for inspiration as to how to actually enter the scores digitally, and started there. I figured my friends wouldn’t be entering their scores themselves anyway, so the first step was a barely-working, clunky prototype that got the job done.
From there, I focused on the backend. Since I could actually start analyzing real bowling scores, I was able to do all sorts of calculations to come up with neat stats - from basic ones like "Overall Average" and "Strike Percentage" to some more abstract ones like "Single-Pin Spare Percentage". With the backend being serviceable, I looked to a front-end charting library that would play well with Angular, eventually settling on ngx-charts. I mocked up a design on how the page itself would look and function now that I knew approximately what I was working with. I came up with various different ways to chart the bowling scores, and wrote down every statistic I had access to that I thought might be interesting to a bowler. As soon as I could, I bulldozed my way to a “minimum viable product” that I could show to my friends. It wasn’t pretty, but it was functional, and that’s what I cared about. It got some “wow”s from my friends and it allowed me to enter scores with relative ease, which was a success in my eyes.
In Conclusion... Sort Of As of the writing of this article, I’m still actively working on this project as I continue to use the tool every week. The page got a major facelift when I learned a bit more about UI design and started converting the site to TailwindCSS, but there is still plenty to work on. Outstanding tasks can be found on my public Trello board where I organize and track all of my tasks and projects. Also, feel free to check out the entire project on GitHub if you're interested!
